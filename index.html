What is the orbital velocity?
The orbital speed of an astronomical body is the speed at which it orbits around either the barycenter or if one body is much more massive than the other bodies of the system combined, it's speed relative to the center of mass of the most massive body.
How to calculate orbital velocity?
To calculate orbital velocity you should use the following formula:
V ≈ (2 * π * a) / T
where:
V = orbital velocity
a = length of the semimajor axis in meters
T = orbital period.
Why use orbital velocity?
The orbital velocity is used in order to achieve orbit around a celestial body.
